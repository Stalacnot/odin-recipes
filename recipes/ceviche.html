Peruvian Ceviche

Ceviche in all of its glory!

Ceviche is a culturally traditional Peruvian dish that brings a great sense of pride among the Peruvians and is celebrated as such.

The "leche de tigre" packs a refreshing punch in this coastal seafood specialty.

**This ingredients list and preperation was found at Cravings Journal! Check them out for more amazing recipes!**

Ingredients

  • 1 kg White Fish Filet
  • Chopped Chili
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • 1/2 Red Onion
  • 40 g Trimmings from Fish Filets
  • 30 g Red or Brown Onions
  • 2 Garlic Cloves
  • Chili
  • 30 g Celery
  • 15 g Ginger
  • Sugar
  • 500 ml Lime Juice
  • 4 Stalks Coriander
  • 2 Cooked Sweet Potatos
  • Cooked Peruvian Corn

Steps

  1. Slice the red onions and let them sit with abundant salt for 15 minutes to soften its flavour. Rinse and reserve.
  2. Cut the fish in 2cm cubes. Any pieces that are too thin go to the leche de tigre.
  3. Mix the fish with the chili, onion, salt and pepper and let it sit while making the leche de tigre.

    4. Leche de Tigre

  4. Squeeze the limes only while the juice comes out easily to prevent from making it bitter from the skin and pit.
  5. Fill the the lime juice filled blender with fish filet trimmings, onion, garlic cloves, chili, celery, ginger, and coriander stalks.
  6. Pulse the blender 3-6 times until everything is in small chunks.
  7. Sift contents through a sieve and press down to get all the juices.
  8. Taste the tiger's milk and adjust the level of salt. Add sugar if it's too sour.
  9. Mix the leche de tigre with the fish and other ingredients and serve immediately with coriander, sweet potato, and Peruvian corn.
